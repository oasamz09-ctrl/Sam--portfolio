User Research & Personas

MySanity AI Behavioural Intelligence Platform

This section presents user insights derived from secondary research, competitor analysis, and behavioural patterns commonly observed across mental wellbeing applications. The findings informed the design of the MySanity AI experience.

Research Approach

Due to the early-stage conceptual nature of this project, user insights were developed using a combination of market research, competitor analysis, and established UX patterns within digital mental health platforms.

  • Analysis of leading mental health apps (Headspace, Calm, Wysa, Finch)
  • Review of common UX patterns in wellbeing and habit-tracking applications
  • Assumptions based on typical user behaviour in stress and anxiety management contexts
  • Evaluation of gaps in existing AI-driven mental health solutions

Key Insights

  • Users often struggle with generic wellbeing advice that lacks personal relevance.
  • Many mental health apps require high cognitive effort, reducing long-term engagement.
  • Users prefer simple, consistent tracking rather than complex multi-step input flows.
  • There is a strong demand for AI-driven personalisation based on behavioural data.
  • Users value emotional safety, tone sensitivity, and non-judgemental feedback.
  • Fragmentation across multiple apps creates friction in maintaining wellbeing routines.

User Personas

Persona 1 — Sarah (Student)

  • Age: 22
  • Context: University student managing anxiety and academic pressure
  • Goals: Improve mental wellbeing, sleep better, build healthy routines
  • Pain Points: Overwhelmed by apps, inconsistent habits, generic advice
  • Needs: Simple tracking, personalised guidance, gentle reminders

Persona 2 — James (Professional)

  • Age: 34
  • Context: Full-time employee experiencing work-related stress
  • Goals: Manage stress, avoid burnout, improve work-life balance
  • Pain Points: Lack of time, fragmented tools, difficulty maintaining routines
  • Needs: Fast insights, voice interaction, minimal effort engagement

Persona 3 — Emily (Healthcare Worker)

  • Age: 29
  • Context: High-pressure healthcare environment with irregular shifts
  • Goals: Build resilience, maintain emotional stability, improve sleep
  • Pain Points: Burnout risk, inconsistent schedule, emotional fatigue
  • Needs: Adaptive AI support, flexible reminders, short wellbeing exercises

Design Implications

  • The system must prioritise low cognitive load interactions to reduce user fatigue.
  • AI recommendations must be personalised using behavioural data such as mood, sleep and activity.
  • The experience should consolidate multiple wellbeing functions into a single platform.
  • Emotional tone and safety must be prioritised in all AI responses.
  • Voice interaction should be supported to reduce friction for time-constrained users.
