MySanity

AI Behavioural Intelligence Platform

A Business Analysis case study designing an AI-powered system that combines mood tracking, sleep data, and conversational AI (text + voice) to generate personalised wellbeing and behavioural insights.

Start Reading Back to Case Studies

Executive Summary

MySanity is an AI-powered behavioural intelligence platform designed to help users understand and improve their wellbeing through structured insights generated from mood tracking, sleep analysis, and conversational AI interactions.

Unlike traditional wellness apps that operate in isolation (sleep tracking, journaling, meditation), MySanity integrates multiple behavioural signals into a single system that uses AI to detect patterns and deliver personalised recommendations.

The platform supports both text and voice-based AI interaction, enabling users to engage naturally while receiving real-time behavioural feedback.

Business Problem

Users today rely on multiple disconnected applications to manage wellbeing: sleep trackers, mood journals, productivity apps, and meditation tools.

These systems do not communicate with each other, resulting in:

  • Fragmented behavioural data across multiple platforms
  • Lack of meaningful insight into patterns (sleep vs mood vs productivity)
  • Generic recommendations with low personalisation
  • Low long-term engagement with wellness apps

As a result, users collect data but do not gain actionable understanding of their behaviour.

Project Objectives

  • Centralise behavioural data (mood, sleep, activity) into one system
  • Enable AI-driven conversational interaction (text + voice)
  • Identify behavioural patterns across time
  • Provide personalised wellbeing recommendations
  • Improve user self-awareness and engagement

My Role (Business Analyst)

I was responsible for defining the business requirements, structuring the product vision, and translating user needs into system-level specifications.

  • Requirements gathering and definition
  • Process mapping and user journey design
  • Feature prioritisation (MVP planning)
  • Stakeholder alignment with developers and AI engineers
  • Defining functional and non-functional requirements
  • Designing AI interaction flows (text + voice)
